The small pink flesh of its nose
hovers about the air.
Its two nostrils like indolent eyes, vacuous
ready to be filled with an aurora of smells
that flies past in the gray-coated rodent’s journey
along the black tarred path.
Its nose turns toward a tower
of brown fabric and black cowhide boots.
On two little feet it stands hunchback
in the manner of a rat majestic,
two little hands as if in prayer
lift a cup of empty air as it hungers on.

looking glass

This black space turns the computer screen into a mirror, whose clarity depends on the brightness and glossiness of the screen. Our focal point when we're online is the center of the screen, with images and texts normally positioned at the middle. But what if this center space becomes a void? Putting the artwork on the side of the screen, away from our instinctive area of focus, forces us to look at what we normally don't see or pay attention to - our own reflection. Though a matte screen may show no discernible reflection, this space then becomes just empty. Like a black canvas. Is there beauty in putting things aside and being gentle on our focus - giving our mind to simply nothing for a while?
