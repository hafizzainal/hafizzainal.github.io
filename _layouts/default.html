Still Life

Pigments
on that blank canvas
once in motion
leaping from the brush
in cobalt and crimson
but look now — the reds
have come to a halt
when noble love sets
in the seat of the heart.

When I Have Come To My Resting Day

The dancers will all stop waltzing
when the music ceases to play,
but until then I do not yet think
I have come to my resting day.

When the music ceases to play,
sit beside me and take away the pain.
I have come to my resting day,
when my breath grows short and faint.

Sit beside me and take away the pain,
remind me of the night we walked by the lake.
My breath grows short and faint
when I laugh at the silly voice you used to make.

Blue

The dew of lonely thoughts
that come about under the
cover of black skies
lingers
on the lip of insanity,
a nine on the litmus
blue, as I am, but not
bruised.

The telecaster that I bought shortly after selling my clarinet that had been keeping dust for months.

Out Of The Clouds

Will you wait for me by the train,
since I’m running late and like an old sock lost,
will you call out my name?

Don’t trust the forecasts, look there’s rain!
You’ll be wet, but I’ll ask the weather to pause
for you, so will you wait for me by the train?

The crowd chattering renders my shouts plain,
I’m looking for your figure, but not a silhouette in sight,
will you call out my name?

The snow is growing thick and thicker, my vision faint.
Like white satin in snow, my senses are freezing in the frost,
will you wait for me by the train?

The icy stab of winter perhaps lays claim
to the flowers of spring. I am both winter and spring,
will you please call out my name?

Out of the clouds in my white gown, chocolate-stained,
I will have forgotten the way, and how soft you look,
but will you still wait for me by the train,
and can I trust you to call out my name?

Fear of Missing

Fear of missing someone is
not calling their phone when
it’s the last time you’ll talk to them
not seeing them for
the last time cancelling that last date
knowing that your heart can’t
take the blow.
It is the lump in your throat
when you try to say goodbye
at the train station
'goodbye' you say
anyway
but it makes your eyes
swell
with tears and makes you
wonder why humans
have affection when it causes more
hurt than the sharp metal you
use to make their favourite tomato soup
at lunches on weekends
and dinners on weekdays.
Fear of missing someone is
fear of someone missing
from your eyes and ears
and your arms.

Lie

The mind treads with hesitant feet
onward, to where the sign reads
‘happiness lies here’.
Happiness does indeed lie there
in a bed of dirt, unmoving, silent.
Her muted cries long gone now, there is
a loneliness in the calm of it all. She was
a ball of happiness and joy, her friends say.
Something broke inside her before she told
her parents she was going to the pizza shop
for a birthday party. She was an honest and
trustworthy one, her parents say. There was
no party. There was no meeting of friends
that night
the doorknob remained unturned.

Cry, Wolf

The vagabond wolf sticks its paw
in the grassy mud, wet from
a fresh storm whose bloody bolts
put to rest five of her cubs,
she stands on the corner
of the cold precipice, waits
for the twinkling moment
to snatch life back
from the wolf gods.

Revelation

The pantalooned prince advances
through steep walls of bookcases
in his breezy stride, feet wide apart
rooted to the white marble floor
he opens the door, and lo
and behold! A revelation—
He who owns everything, owns
everything but the soul
of a poet, for the poet’s
heart is bought not with
gold, but with the gift of words.

Rust

Rust the colour of worn-out copper
obscures the glimmer of moving spirit inside
the old cog. Appears solid, not brittle or
vulnerable to breaking on touch. It sits neatly
between ribs of unshakeable construct like
the heart does, showing signs of wear – Good. Its
potential has been run through and exhausted.
It is history that is here.

On Damascus Steel

On Damascus steel the city
runs its business
plucking flowers from gardens
lumping colourful shirts, dresses
to make fuel for the burning
the burning of the river of growth.

Lover's Flame

The flame burns brighter in total darkness
like the sun in all of black space
where there is but one mighty fire flickering
—a candle painting a woman’s face
amber as her eyes pour out water from the heart’s crevasse
mouth mumbling goodbye in one last lover’s embrace.

Rat's Plight

The small pink flesh of its nose
hovers about the air.
Its two nostrils like indolent eyes, vacuous
ready to be filled with an aurora of smells
that flies past in the gray-coated rodent’s journey
along the black tarred path.
Its nose turns towards a tower
of brown fabric and black cowhide boots.
On two little feet it stands hunchback
in the manner of a rat majestic,
two little hands as if in prayer
lift a cup of empty air as it hungers on.

Lullaby

At this dull hour I hear
born from the midnight silence
a song, so sweet like honey
inviting my weary body to lie down
to sleep and dream of
an eternity.
And the clock ceases its ticking
here
my waking eyes welcome the light
as I rise in a gentle breeze
caressing my naked shoulders
and in the distance
over the water I make out
a fatherly figure, a boatman swaying
his oar, coming towards me and singing
that familiar lullaby so
charming it makes me immortal
now
on the floor like a baby
I sleep
as the morning bird cheeps
cheeps
