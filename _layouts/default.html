Out of the Clouds

Will you wait for me by the train,
since I’m running late and like an old sock lost,
will you call out my name?

Don’t trust the forecasts, look there’s rain!
You’ll be wet, but I’ll ask the weather to pause
for you, so will you wait for me by the train?

The crowd chattering renders my shouts plain,
I’m looking for your figure, but not a silhouette in sight,
will you call out my name?

The snow is growing thick and thicker, my vision faint.
Like white satin in snow, my senses are freezing in the frost,
will you wait for me by the train?

The icy stab of winter perhaps lays claim
to the flowers of spring. I am both winter and spring,
will you please call out my name?

Out of the clouds in my white gown, chocolate-stained,
I will have forgotten the way, and how soft you look,
but will you still wait for me by the train,
and can I trust you to call out my name?

Fear of Missing

Fear of missing someone is
not calling their phone when
it’s the last time you’ll talk to them
not seeing them for
the last time cancelling that last date
knowing that your heart can’t
take the blow.
It is the lump in your throat
when you try to say goodbye
at the train station
'goodbye' you say
anyway
but it makes your eyes
swell
with tears and makes you
wonder why humans
have affection when it causes more
hurt than the sharp metal you
use to make their favourite tomato soup
at lunches on weekends
and dinners on weekdays.
Fear of missing someone is
fear of someone missing
from your eyes and ears
and your arms.

Lie

The mind treads with hesitant feet
onward, to where the sign reads
‘happiness lies here’.
Happiness does indeed lie there
in a bed of dirt, unmoving, silent.
Her muted cries long gone now, there is
a loneliness in the calm of it all. She was
a ball of happiness and joy, her friends say.
Something broke inside her before she told
her parents she was going to the pizza shop
for a birthday party. She was an honest and
trustworthy one, her parents say. There was
no party. There was no meeting of friends
that night
the doorknob remained unturned.

Rust

Rust the colour of worn-out copper
obscures the glimmer of moving spirit inside
the old cog. Appears solid, not brittle or
vulnerable to breaking on touch. It sits neatly
between ribs of unshakeable construct like
the heart does, showing signs of wear – Good. Its
potential has been run through and exhausted.
It is history that is here.

Demon Dayz festival - one of the best days of my life. I bought a ticket on a whim after being persuaded by Sam. At the time (around March 2017) I wasn't yet a big fan of Gorillaz or any other Damon Albarn's bands although I remember loving a few of their songs which Sam introduced me to. As our friendship progressed so did my appreciation for Gorillaz and not much later, for Blur and Damon Albarn too. I only realised that they had been a major part of my life after finding out my top 5 most played songs in 2017 were all from Gorillaz and Blur. I also found out I had listened to over 50k minutes (833 hours) of music in the year 2017. I am appreciative of Sam for having introduced me to Gorillaz and Blur, whose music have helped me in difficult times and intensified feelings of joy when I did feel joyful.
